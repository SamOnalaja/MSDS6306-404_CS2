Executive Summary

Talent management is an iterative process of developing and retaining employees. Our analysis yielded that there are ten (10) factors that contribute to turnover with an overall 87.3% accuracy. While there were positive relationships between the varying satisfaction metrics recently collected by job roles, the differences were not statistically significant.

Introduction

DDSAnalytics is tasked with predicting employee turnover by conducting an analysis of employee data for the purpose of talent management. While seeking to determine factors that lead to attrition and identify job role specific trends, our deliverable is to build a model to predict attrition with at least 60% accuracy.

Prediction Conclusion

With an 87.3% overall accuracy, our custom regression model identified that business travel, commute distance, environment satisfaction, job involvement, job role, job satisfaction, marital status, number of companies worked, overtime, and relationship satisfaction are factors that contribute to attrition. If instead one wishes for a slightly less complex model, another one was created with 85% accuracy. This model contains half the number of terms (five): job involvement, overtime, marital status, job role, and number of companies worked for. Managing these factors in developing and retaining employees could reduce and prevent attrition.
